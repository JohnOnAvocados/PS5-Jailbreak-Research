## Summary
ENISA is the European Union Agency for Cybersecurity, dedicated to achieving a high common level of cybersecurity across Europe. It supports EU member states and private sector entities through cyber threat analysis, risk management guidance, certification frameworks, incident response coordination, and vulnerability disclosure programs. Key operational areas include the European Vulnerability Database, NIS2 directive support, Cyber Europe exercises, and the National Cybersecurity Assessment Framework (NCAF).

## Key Concepts
- EU Agency for Cybersecurity established to support member states
- Cyber Europe exercises for cross-border incident response
- European Vulnerability Database (EUVD)
- NIS2 directive implementation support
- National Cybersecurity Assessment Framework (NCAF 2.0)
- Cybersecurity certification frameworks (CRA notified bodies)
- Threat landscape and state-of-cybersecurity reporting
- Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) adoption tracking

## Security Relevance
Provides the European regulatory and operational framework for cybersecurity that governs threat modeling requirements for products sold in the EU. The Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) and certification frameworks directly impact the security requirements for trusted systems.
