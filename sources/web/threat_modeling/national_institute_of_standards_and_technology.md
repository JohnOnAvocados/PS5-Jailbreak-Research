## Summary
The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is the US federal measurement standards laboratory responsible for developing measurement standards, cybersecurity frameworks, and technology guidelines. Key cybersecurity outputs include the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF), NIST SP 800 series (risk management, security controls), National Vulnerability Database (NVD), cryptographic standards (FIPS), post-quantum cryptography initiatives, and the AI Risk Management Framework. NIST operates under the Department of Commerce.

## Key Concepts
- US National Metrology Institute (NMI)
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- NIST SP 800 series security and privacy publications
- National Vulnerability Database (NVD) integration
- Cryptographic standards and FIPS publications
- Post-quantum cryptography standardization
- AI Risk Management Framework and AI consortiums
- CHIPS for America initiative (semiconductor manufacturing)
- Computer Security Resource Center (CSRC)

## Security Relevance
NIST produces the foundational standards and guidelines that define security requirements for all US government systems and broadly influence industry practice. The CSF, SP 800 series, and cryptographic standards set the technical baseline that any trusted system must meet for US compliance and security assurance.
