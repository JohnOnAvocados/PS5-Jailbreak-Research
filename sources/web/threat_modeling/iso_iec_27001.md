## Summary
ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). It specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an ISMS. Based on the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), it provides a risk management framework for securing information in all forms (paper, cloud, digital). The standard is applicable to organizations of any size and sector, with over 70,000 certificates issued worldwide across 150 countries.

## Key Concepts
- Information Security Management System (ISMS) requirements
- CIA triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
- Risk management process for information security
- Organization-wide protection covering people, policies, technology
- Jointly published by ISO and IEC (JTC 1/SC 27)
- Supports certification by accredited conformity assessment bodies
- Edition 3 published 2022, with 2024 Amendment 1 (climate action)

## Security Relevance
Establishes the international benchmark for information security governance that any trusted system must align with. The ISMS framework provides the risk management discipline for systematically identifying and addressing security weaknesses in system design and operation.
