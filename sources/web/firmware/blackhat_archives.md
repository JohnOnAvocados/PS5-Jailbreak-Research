## Summary
Black Hat is a premier information security conference series featuring briefings on cutting-edge security research including firmware exploitation, hardware attacks, boot security, and trusted platform vulnerabilities. The archives contain presentation materials and whitepapers from past conferences covering topics such as UEFI exploits, Intel ME/AMD PSP attacks, TPM bypass techniques, secure boot bypasses, and console jailbreak research. Presentations are categorized by year and region (USA, Europe, Asia).

## Key Concepts
- Firmware and hardware exploitation presentations
- UEFI and BIOS attack techniques
- Intel ME and AMD PSP security research
- TPM and secure element attack vectors
- Secure boot and measured boot bypasses
- Console and embedded system exploitation
- Boot ROM and early initialization attacks

## Security Relevance
Archives serve as a repository of disclosed firmware and hardware vulnerabilities affecting the trusted computing base. Black Hat presentations frequently disclose novel attack techniques against platform firmware, boot chains, and hardware security mechanisms, directly informing trust model analysis.
