## Summary
USENIX is a nonprofit organization advancing computing systems research through conferences, publications, and community building. It hosts top-tier venues including USENIX Security Symposium, OSDI (Operating Systems Design and Implementation), and ATC (Annual Technical Conference). USENIX maintains an open-access policy — all published research is freely available. Its conferences are leading venues for CPU architecture security, trusted execution systems, OS-level isolation, and vulnerability research.

## Key Concepts
- USENIX Security — top security conference (hardware security, TEE attacks)
- OSDI — premier OS/systems conference (hardware-software co-design)
- ATC — systems research including hypervisor and container isolation
- WOOT — Workshop on Offensive Technologies (exploitation, hardware attacks)

## Security Relevance
USENIX venues regularly publish breakthrough research on CPU security including Spectre/Meltdown variants, TEE attacks (SGX, SEV, TrustZone), and new trusted hardware architectures. Its open access ensures that trusted system model research — both offensive and defensive — is freely available. OSDI and USENIX Security are central to understanding OS-level vs. hardware-level trust boundaries.
