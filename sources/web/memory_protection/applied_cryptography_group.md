## Summary
The Stanford Applied Cryptography Group, led by Professor Dan Boneh, conducts research on network and computer security with a focus on applications of cryptography. Research areas include lattice-based cryptography, privacy-preserving systems, blockchain technologies, pairing-based cryptography, and web security. The group has produced foundational work on memory safety (ASLR effectiveness analysis, PaX randomization attacks) through researchers like Hovav Shacham.

## Key Concepts
- led by Dan Boneh, a leading figure in applied cryptography
- research covers lattice cryptography, pairings, zero-knowledge proofs, and homomorphic encryption
- published seminal paper on the effectiveness of address-space randomization (Shacham et al., CCS 2004)
- projects include Bulletproofs, Prio, Balloon Hashing, and Riposte
- part of the Stanford Security Lab
- research on web security, SSL/TLS, and OS security
- CS251 course on blockchain technologies

## Security Relevance
The group published one of the foundational analyses of ASLR effectiveness (Shacham et al., ACM CCS 2004), demonstrating that 16 bits of address randomization can be defeated by brute force within minutes. This work directly influenced modern ASLR entropy requirements and informed the design of memory protection in trusted systems.
