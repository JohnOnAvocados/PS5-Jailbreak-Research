## Summary
USENIX is the Advanced Computing Systems Association, a nonprofit organization that hosts leading systems conferences including USENIX Security Symposium, OSDI (Operating Systems Design and Implementation), ATC (Annual Technical Conference), and SREcon. It is a vendor-neutral venue for groundbreaking systems research including hypervisor design, container security, trusted execution environments, and operating system security. All USENIX publications are freely accessible via open access.

## Security Relevance
USENIX Security and OSDI are premier venues for hypervisor and trusted systems research. Landmark papers on hypervisor isolation, side-channel attacks on virtualization, TEE security, and kernel hardening have been published at USENIX venues. The open-access model ensures broad availability of security-critical systems research.
