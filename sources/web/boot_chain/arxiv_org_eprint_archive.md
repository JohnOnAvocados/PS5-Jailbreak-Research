## Summary
arXiv is a free distribution service and open-access archive for nearly 2.4 million scholarly articles in physics, mathematics, computer science, quantitative biology, quantitative finance, statistics, electrical engineering and systems science, and economics. Materials are not peer-reviewed by arXiv (moderation only). The Computer Science section includes Cryptography and Security (cs.CR), Hardware Architecture (cs.AR), and Operating Systems (cs.OS) categories relevant to boot chain research. arXiv provides rapid dissemination of preprints in secure boot, trusted computing, firmware security, and TPM analysis before or alongside formal peer review.

## Security Relevance
arXiv is a major source for early/pre-publication research on boot chain vulnerabilities, secure boot implementations, TPM attacks, and firmware security. Many foundational papers appear on arXiv before conference publication.
