## Summary
USENIX is a nonprofit computing organization that hosts conferences including USENIX Security Symposium, USENIX Operating Systems Design and Implementation (OSDI), USENIX Annual Technical Conference (ATC), and WOOT (Workshop on Offensive Technologies). USENIX maintains an open access policy—conference materials and research are free to publish and free to read. Proceedings cover systems security, operating systems, storage, networking, and offensive security research including firmware exploitation, trusted execution environments, and platform security.

## Key Concepts

- Open access research publication policy
- Systems security and platform trust research
- Operating systems and firmware interface research
- Vendor-neutral computing community

## Security Relevance
USENIX Security is one of the top academic security conferences, regularly publishing firmware-level attack research, trusted execution environment vulnerabilities, and platform security analyses. WOOT focuses on offensive security techniques including hardware and firmware exploitation. USENIX proceedings are a key source for understanding disclosed trust model vulnerabilities.
